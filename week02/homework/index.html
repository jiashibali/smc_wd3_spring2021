Tea is an aromatic beverage commonly prepared by pouring hot or boiling water over cured or fresh leaves of Camellia sinensis, an evergreen shrub native to East Asia. After water, it is the most widely consumed drink in the world.

BLACK TEA

Black teas come in dark brown and black leaf appearance and generally stronger in flavor than other teas. Process: wilted, sometimes crushed, and fully oxidized

GREEN TEA

Green teas are typically steeped for shorter amounts of time and at lower temperatures which will produce a lighter cup with less caffeine. Process: unwilted and unoxidized

OOLONG TEA

Oolong teas can be sweet and fruity with honey aromas, or woody and thick with roasted aromas, or green and fresh with complex aromas. Process: wilted, bruised, and partially oxidized

WHITE TEA

White teas are generally young or minimally processed leaves resulting in a flavor characterized as "lighter" than other teas. Process: wilted and unoxidized

YELLOW TEA

Yellow teas are processed with the aim of removing the characteristic grassy smell of green tea. Process: unwilted and unoxidized but allowed to yellow

DARK TEA

Dark teas, or also known as fermented teas, undergo microbial fermentation, from several months to many years. Process: green tea that has been allowed to ferment/compost
