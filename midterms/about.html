Our History

Serving unique mincemeat filling since 1987

Mincemeat International endured as an American and European tradition for decades, whether a holiday dessert or a classic family favorite for any season all year round. Mincemeat is a combination of apples, raisins and citrus peel, blended with sugar and spices to make a delicious cooking and baking ingredient. But in Mincemeat International, we take pride in making unique flavors that aren't used traditionally. So if you're feeling adventurous and want to try something different, we have unique flavors that goes down as a treat!

It's called mincemeat but there's no meat in it

The reason mincemeat is called meat is because that’s exactly what it used to be: most often mutton, but also beef, rabbit, pork or game. Mince pies were first served in the early middle ages, and the pies were quite sizeable, filled with a mixture of finely minced meat, chopped up fruit and a preserving liquid. Today’s mince pies bear little resemblance to the early versions, but as the recipes, methods and ingredients available have developed over the years, we have ended up with a tasty treat using the finest ingredients, made in the same way it has been for years – but quite a difference from the original recipes driven more by necessity than by delight.
